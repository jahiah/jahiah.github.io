The Birthday Paradox Project

Motivation of the project

The Birthday Paradox is a phenomenon where it is likely that two people within a group share a birthday. This phenomenon increases as the amount of people within the group increases.

Functionality

This project simulates the Birthday Paradox by running experiments with groups of 5 to 100 people, incrementing by 5. Each experiment runs 20 trials and checks if at least two people share a birthday.

Project design

The four classes within the project include: Simulation.java: Runs experiments and prints the results. Experiment.java: Manages the experiment and unning trials for different group sizes. Trial.java: Simulates a single trial and checking if two people share a birthday. Birthday.java: Generates a valid random birthday for each person.

Implementation

I worked on the Experiment.java, Trial.java, and Birthday.java classes. The Experiment class runs multiple trials for each group size. While the Trial class checks if two people in a group share the same birthday. I also implementated test-driven development.
